# Matricaria recutita (German Chamomile)

## Botanical Information
- **Family:** Asteraceae
- **Parts Used:** Flowers (flowering tops)
- **Energetics:** Cooling, drying, bitter

## Indications
- **Digestive:** Dyspepsia, flatulence, colic, inflammatory conditions of GI tract
- **Nervous:** Anxiety, insomnia, restlessness, mild sedative
- **Inflammatory:** Skin inflammation, wound healing, eczema
- **Gynecological:** Dysmenorrhea, menopausal symptoms
- **General:** Febrile conditions, teething pain in children

## Contraindications
- Hypersensitivity to Asteraceae family (ragweed, daisies)
- Pregnancy (high doses may stimulate uterus)
- Concurrent use with CNS depressants (additive effect)

## Preparations
- **Tea:** 1-2 tsp dried flowers per cup, steep 5-10 minutes
- **Tincture:** 1:5 in 45% alcohol, 1-4 ml TID
- **Essential oil:** Topical use only, highly concentrated

## Evidence
- Traditional use in European herbal medicine (systematic)
- Clinical trials support anxiolytic effects
- In vitro studies confirm anti-inflammatory (bisabolol)

## Drug Interactions
- CYP450 substrate - may interact with drugs metabolized by liver
- Additive sedative effects with benzodiazepines
- May increase bleeding risk with anticoagulants (theoretical)

## Safety Notes
- Generally recognized as safe (GRAS) by FDA
- Rare allergic reactions in sensitive individuals
- Essential oil should not be used internally
